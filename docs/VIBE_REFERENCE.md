# Vibe reference
*Derived, non-authoritative. A physical-world reference chosen by Anthony that the night mode should feel like. If it conflicts with canon, canon wins — but it doesn't: this photo is the canon's palette found in the wild.*

The reference is a photograph of a small whisky bar at night (`docs/reference/bar.jpg` — add the photo there). What it teaches, translated into interface terms:

**Light behaves like this.** A few small warm sources — two mushroom lamps, a strip of amber under the bar — and everything else falls into comfortable darkness. Light pools; it doesn't fill. In the app: the accent hairlines and any glow are small and local, exactly like those lamps. Nothing is evenly lit. Brightness is spent, not applied.

**Darkness is most of the frame, and it's warm.** The ceiling and floor are near-black with brown in it, never gray or blue. Matches Room `#131110`. The dark parts of a screen are not empty space waiting for content — they are the room.

**The amber is the photo's Lamplight.** The under-bar glow and the wood grain in lamplight sit right at `#D9A441`. Note what the photo does with it: amber occupies maybe 10% of the frame and feels abundant. Our 5% ceiling will feel like this bar if the accent is placed where light would actually fall — edges, undersides, rims — never as a fill.

**Materials are matte and worn.** Brick, turned wood, old stools. Nothing glossy except glass catching light — in the app, the only "shine" belongs to photographs, never to chrome. Paper grain at 3–5% is our version of this room's texture.

**Detail lives in the lit band.** The bottle shelves are dense with detail, but only where the light is; the eye rests everywhere else. In the app: content (the entries, the serif) sits in the lit band; UI recedes into the dark like the stools in the foreground.

**One warm accent among the warm.** The single red-orange lamp on the bar reads as an event because everything around it is quiet amber and brown. That is how Overlap `#8A6A6E` should land: rare, small, and only meaningful because the rest is restrained.

**The feeling to preserve:** late, unhurried, slightly hushed, a place two people would talk for three hours. If a screen wouldn't belong in this bar, redesign it.

## For Claude Code
When building or reviewing any night-mode screen, load this file and (if present) `docs/reference/bar.jpg`. Judge screenshots by the question: *does this screen feel like a corner of that bar?*
